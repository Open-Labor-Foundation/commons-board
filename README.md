# commons-board

*What you know lifts us all.*

AI-powered governance for any organization, backed by the specialist knowledge of [labor-commons](https://github.com/Open-Labor-Foundation/labor-commons).

Whether you're a sole business owner who needs expert guidance in domains outside your field, or a worker collective running shared operations — commons-board gives you a full organizational structure staffed by specialists from the catalog. Legal, accounting, HR, IT, marketing, operations: all covered, all drawn from labor-commons, all available from day one.

commons-board is self-hosted and free (AGPL-3.0). It never charges you to use it. In business mode it gives you the full commercial stack to bill your *own* customers; in collective mode it gives you a pooled treasury and governed distribution.

> **Status: in development.** commons-board is a full migration and transformation of a prior AI executive-board platform into the OLF stack. The complete design and the phased implementation plan live in [planning/](planning/) — start with [planning/concept.md](planning/concept.md) and [planning/execution-plan.md](planning/execution-plan.md).

## Two governance modes

| Mode | Who holds authority | Economic engine |
|---|---|---|
| **Business** | The owner / founding team | Commercial monetization — bill your own customers (subscriptions, per-seat, invoicing) |
| **Collective** | The membership (votes, consensus, amendments) | Pooled treasury and governed distribution |

One platform, one engine. The mode determines who approves what — everything else is shared.

---

## How it works

commons-board runs an organization through a governed hierarchy:

- **Board** — strategy, policy, and cross-function coordination
- **Executive chairs** — functional ownership across finance, marketing, operations, legal, HR, and product
- **Orchestrating agents** — coordinate multi-step workflows across departments
- **Worker agents** — execute specific tasks within a defined scope

Each level has defined authority. No agent acts outside its granted scope.

---

## Governance modes

| Mode | Behavior |
|---|---|
| **Advisor** | Every proposed action surfaces for human approval before executing |
| **Orchestrator** | Routine actions execute automatically; novel or high-risk actions surface for approval |
| **Autopilot** | All actions execute within policy thresholds; low-confidence decisions escalate automatically |

Start in Advisor mode. Move to Orchestrator or Autopilot as trust is established.
